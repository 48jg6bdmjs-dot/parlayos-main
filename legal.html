Understanding a "73% model" pick — why it isn't a 73% chance to win your parlay

A per-leg model percentage describes ParlayOS's estimate for that one game or prop only. It is not the chance that a parlay built from several such legs will pay out — combining legs multiplies their probabilities together, which is why parlay win rates drop off far faster than the individual numbers suggest. See the About page for a worked comparison, including why the site's own founder stakes larger, higher-leg parlays at a fraction of a normal unit (informally, "dime" or ten-cent-style staking) rather than a full unit — precisely because the realistic hit rate on a 7+ leg parlay is low even when every individual leg looks favorable.
