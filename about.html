About ParlayOS

ParlayOS is a picks and analytics tool built by someone who bets his own parlays and got tired of staking them all the same way. This page exists to make one thing unmistakably clear before you subscribe: a model percentage on a single game is not the same number as your chance of winning a parlay built from several of them.

A "73% model" pick is not a 73% parlay

Real math, not a marketing number — same formula used by the app's own parlay probability calculator.

Every percentage you see next to a single pick in ParlayOS — the "73.1% model" pill, an edge percentage, an O/U confidence bar — describes that one leg only. It is the model's estimate that that specific game or prop lands the way ParlayOS thinks it will. It says nothing on its own about a parlay.

A parlay only pays if every single leg hits. Combining legs means multiplying their probabilities together, and that number falls off far faster than most people expect. Even stacking eight genuinely strong 65% picks together — not coin flips, real model-favored picks — leaves you with roughly a 3% chance the whole ticket cashes. At a more typical 57%-per-leg favorite, eight legs is closer to 1-in-90. This isn't a flaw in the model; it's what "AND-ing together" several real, independent probabilities always does. Why the app's own picks get staked smaller as parlays get longer

The founder's approach to sizing bets on long parlays

Because the real odds above are what they are, the person who built ParlayOS does not stake a 12-leg parlay the same way he'd stake a straight bet or a 2-leg parlay. Once a parlay stretches past around 6 legs, he sizes it at a small fraction of his normal unit — informally, betting it "in dimes" (roughly a tenth of a full stake) rather than a full unit — specifically because the realistic hit rate on a 7+ leg ticket is low even when every individual leg genuinely looks good.

This is standard bankroll-management practice among disciplined parlay bettors generally, not something unique to ParlayOS — long parlays are treated as a small, high-variance slice of a bankroll, not a core strategy sized like a normal bet.
